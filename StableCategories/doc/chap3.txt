  [1X3.3 [33X[0;0YStable categories defined by systems of lifting objects[133X[101X

  [1X3.4 [33X[0;0YStable categories defined by systems of colifting objects[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet  [23X\CC[123X  be a category equipped with a system [23X\QQ[123X of colifting objects. The
  set of all morphisms [23X\alpha:A \to B[123X which colift along [23Xq_A:A \to Q_A[123X defines
  a  two-sided  ideal  [23XI_{\QQ}[123X  of  morphisms  in  [23X\CC[123X. It can be shown that a
  morphism  [23X\alpha:  A  \to  B[123X  belongs  to  [23XI_{\QQ}[123X if and only if it factors
  through at least on object in [23X\QQ[123X. The extra structure which is bundled with
  the  system  of  colifting  objects  enables  us  to lift more than additive
  structure  form  [23X\CC[123X  to  [23X\CC/I_{\LL}[123X.  The  category  [23X\CC/{I_{\QQ}}[123X  can be
  constructed by the following operation:[133X
  
  [1X3.4-1 StableCategoryByClassOfColiftingObjects[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XStableCategoryByClassOfColiftingObjects[102X( [3Xcategory[103X ) [32X attribute[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya stable category[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  argument  is  an  additive category equipped with a system of colifting
  objects  [23X\QQ[123X. The output is the stable category [23X\CC/I_{\QQ}[123X where [23XI_{\QQ}[123X is
  the  two-sided  ideal  of morphisms [23X\alpha: A \to B[123X in [23X\CC[123X that colift along
  [23Xq_A:A \to Q_A[123X.[133X
